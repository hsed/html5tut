Introduction to HTML5

What is HTML5?

The gateway of the modern era, HTML5 is slowly and steadily changing the way we interact, visualise and access information on the web. HTML5 is the fifth version of the de facto mark-up language (HTML) which is used to display content on a webpage.



In the previous tutorial you were introduced to the syntax of HTML. Over the past decade developers around the world that are part of the W3C (World Wide Web Consortium) have significantly enhanced the standard language (HTML4) bringing it newer capabilities especially in areas such as multimedia, responsiveness and graphics. This edition (V5) is still under development and newer features are finalized every few months. Simply put, HTML5 has made creating amazing websites easier than ever before.

Some Benefits of HTML5


  • HTML5 does not require any kind of third-party installation on the local computer or the web browser, it just works as long as the browser supports and recognizes it.
  • Developers can very easily create native 2D games using vectors as well as graphics with support of the CANVAS tag. The game performance is automatically adjusted by the browser depending on computer specs so that a wide variety of users can play it without overloading their computer.
  • Animated content especially mouse hover animations can be very easily created which will be later discussed in this section and the next (CSS3).
  • Easy access to storage capabilities for saving settings and a users’ progress.
  • Video and audio (format dependant) can be easily play-backed on a browser without the need of third party plugins such as Adobe® Flash.
